Graveside services for Helen Maxine Robinson will be 2:00 p.m. Monday, April 21, 2008, at the Waynoka Municipal Cemetery with Rev. John Brint, pastor of the Waynoka Church of God officiating.  Interment is under the direction of Marshall Funeral Home of Waynoka, LLC.  
Helen Maxine Robinson, daughter of the late Clarence Lee and Jessie (Moore) Cox, was born September 16, 1919, at Kingman, Kansas, and passed away April 18, 2008, at her home in Waynoka, Oklahoma, at the age of 88 years, 7 months, and 12 days.  
Since she grew up during the depression, she attended school wherever her father could find work.  On September 18, 1958, she was united in marriage to William Lee Robinson at Cashville, Missouri.  They lived in California where she managed the Five Points Laundry and Cleaners.  They then moved to Alma, Missouri where they bought a hardware store.  In 1972, they returned to Waynoka where she worked at the E. P. Clapper Hospital.  
She was an active member of the Waynoka Church of God.  She enjoyed sewing and baking.  
Besides her parents, she was preceded in death by one daughter, Joyce Ann Robinson; one brother, Clarence Cox; and three sisters, Myrtle Cooper, Elizabeth Meyers, and Dorthy Gaskill.  
Helen is survived by her husband, Bill; three sons, Ronnie Lee Robinson and his wife, Charlene, of Brownwood, Texas, Ricky Eugene Robinson of Topeka, Kansas, and Donnie Gaskill of Winfield, Kansas; four daughters, Nancy Lee Woodard of Waynoka, Roberta Mae Curtis of Annabella, Utah, Dolores Maxine Zook of Waynoka, and Kathryn Forcum of Red Bluff, California; eleven grandchildren, Ricky, Gerald, Helen, David, Will, Les, Glenda, Marilee, Valerie, Bryan, Stacy; twelve great grandchildren, Summer, Krystal, Helen, Angel, April, Christine, Stefan, Michael, Casey, Justin, Tyler, Lisa; other relatives and friends.  
Memorial contributions may be made through the funeral home to the Waynoka EMTs.
