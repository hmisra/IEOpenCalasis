Funeral services for Henry Dean Evans will be 2:00 p.m. Saturday, January 23, 2010, at the Alva Church of God with Pastor Bob Brown officiating.  Interment will be in the Alva Municipal Cemetery under the direction of Marshall Funeral Home of Alva.  There will be a visitation with the family at the funeral home Friday from 6:00 p.m. to 7:30 p.m. 	 Henry Dean Evans, son of the late Walter Melvin and Wilma Ada (Brace) Evans, was born August 16, 1927, at Alva, Oklahoma, and passed away January 19, 2010, at Clinton, Oklahoma at the age of 82 years, 5 months, and 3 days. 	 Dean attended the West Point School before graduating from Waynoka High School.  He then served in the United States Army and was stationed in Japan.  	 On October 10, 1953, he was united in marriage to Erlene Marie Coulter at the Baptist Church in Waynoka.  They lived in Alva most of his life where he worked as a carpenter for Henson Construction, Egner Construction and Dan Evans Construction. 	 He was a member of the Alva Church of God, a past Commander and Life Member of Alva VFW Post #2847, and the Waynoka Saddle Club.  He loved being outdoors where he hunted, fished, and tended his garden.  He and Erlene also loved square dancing. 	 Besides his parents, he was preceded in death by one brother, Dan Evans. 	 Dean is survived by his wife, Erlene, of Alva; one son, Melvin Ray Evans and wife, Sherry, of Alva; one daughter, Carolyn Jean Evans of Alva; one brother, Lester Evans and wife, Imogene, of Waynoka; two sisters, Norma Wilhite of Hardtner, Kansas and Edith Haight of Alva; one sister-in-law, Ella Evans of Alva; one granddog Romeo; other relatives and many friends. 	 Memorial contributions may be made through the funeral home to the Alva Church of God Building Fund.
