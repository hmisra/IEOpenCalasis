Graveside services for Debra Campbell will be 10:00 a.m., Monday, December 22, 2014, at the Avard Cemetery. Interment is under the direction of Marshall Funeral Home of Alva.    
  Debra Sue Campbell was born November 15, 1954, to Kenneth and Ramona (Brown) Campbell at Cherokee, Oklahoma, and passed away December 18, 2014, at Oklahoma City, Oklahoma, at the age of 60 years, 1 month, and 3 days.    
  Debra attended school in Cherokee. In 1993 Deb moved to Avard where she and her partner of 21 years, Nan Wheatley owned and operated Vina Rae’s Café. In 2001 Deb fell ill to the flesh eating bacteria and spent a year in the hospital in Enid. She was handicapped from this and spent her remaining years in the home where her partner took care of her.    
  Her favorite pastime was her computer, meeting friends on Facebook and Farmville. She dearly loved her two fur babies, Rudy and Petey.    
  Besides her parents, Kenny and Ramona, she is preceded in death by three brothers, Jim, David, and Bill Blain; and one sister, Mildred Green.    
  Surviving are her partner, Nan, of Avard; two special cousins, Ronda Inman of Enid, and Sondra Shafer of Nescatunga; one brother, Bob Blain and his wife, Rae, of Liberal, Kansas; one sister, Pat Rosell and her husband, Benny, of Baldwin City, Kansas; nieces and nephews, other relatives and friends.    
  In lieu of flowers memorial contributions may be made through the funeral home for funeral expenses.
