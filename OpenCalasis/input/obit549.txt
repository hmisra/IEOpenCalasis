Funeral services for Don Daryl Denny (D.D.D.) will be 10:00 a.m. Thursday, March 12, 2009, at the Marshall Funeral Home Chapel with Rev. Bob Brown officiating. Arrangements are under the direction of Marshall Funeral Home.  At Don?s request he will be cremated following the service.  
Don Daryl Denny, son of the late James Preston  and Emma (Quimby) Denny was born May 24, 1922, at Wellington, Kansas.  He passed away March 8,  2009, at Alva, Oklahoma, at the age of 86 years, 9 months, and 12 days.  
Don graduated from Wellington High School and went to college at the University of Oklahoma, Tulsa University, and Ft. Hays State University.  On April 7, 1950, he was united in marriage to Mercedes Butts at Woodward, Oklahoma.  During the war, he volunteered to serve in the United States Navy.  After his discharge, he went to work for the Santa Fe Railroad, starting as a brakeman before becoming a conductor.  They lived in Wellington, Kansas before moving to Alva in 1997.  
He was a member of the Methodist Church.  He was a 32nd Degree Mason, and belonged to the American Legion and Veterans of Foreign Wars in Wellington.  He enjoyed boating, football, and spending time with his grandchildren.  
Besides his parents, he was preceded in death by his wife, Mercedes; one brother, two sisters, and one great granddaughter.  
Don is survived by his daughter, Mary Ann Crow and her husband, Wally, of Alva; four grandchildren, Dennis Crow and his wife, Shannon, Cynthia Hitt and her husband, Ray, and Timothy Crow all of Pratt, Kansas, and Melody Hofer of Alva, Oklahoma; 12 great grandchildren; 16 great great grandchildren; other relatives and friends.  
Memorial contributions may be made through the funeral home to the Cerebral Palsy Foundation.
