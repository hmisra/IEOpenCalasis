Funeral services for Kimberly Kay Strait will be 2:00 p.m. Thursday, October 16, 2008, at the First Baptist Church with Rev. William S. Kalfas, of the Alva Church of the Nazarene, officiating. Interment will be in the Alva Municipal Cemetery under the direction of Marshall Funeral Home. 	 Kimberly Kay (Koppitz) Strait, daughter of Dick Koppitz and Peggy (Seeger) Mackey, was born January 5, 1960, in Alva, Oklahoma, and passed away October 12, 2008, in Alva, Oklahoma, at the age of 48 years, 9 months, 7 days. 	 Kim grew up in Alva and graduated from Alva High School with the class of 1978. She went two years to Northwestern Oklahoma State University before attending Business School in Oklahoma City. She lived in Moore, Oklahoma City, and Nash before returning to Alva in 2000. 	 She was a member of the First Baptist Church. Kim was an avid OU fan, was an accomplished barrel racer in high school, and won a saddle for her showmanship as an 8 year old.  She was selected Waynoka Rodeo Queen in 1969.  Attending her son, Logan?s sporting events was a highlight of hers. 	 Kim was married to Lewis Ogle and later married Ed Strait.  Both preceded her in death. 	 Kim is survived by her son, Logan Ogle, of Alva; her father, Dick Koppitz, of Hardtner, Kansas, her mother and stepfather, Peggy and Lee Mackey; a close friend, Janet Schwerdtfeger, of Alva; a stepbrother, Mike Mackey and his wife, Michelle, of Alva, three stepsisters, Lynda Culbertson and her husband, Greg, of Dallas, Texas, Marcia Martin and her husband, Mike, of Alva, and Dana Foote and her husband, Tim, of Clovis, New Mexico. 	 Also surviving are aunt and uncles, Patsy and Troy O?Hair, of Alva, and Harold Koppitz of Alva; a host of nieces and nephews, cousins, other relatives and many friends. 	 Memorial contributions may be made through the funeral home to Oklahoma Medical Research Foundation for heart or cancer research or Creature Concerns.
