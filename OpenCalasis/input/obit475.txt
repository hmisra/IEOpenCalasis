Funeral services for Virgil Lyle Murrow will be 10:00 a.m. Saturday, May 29, 2010, at the Alva First Baptist Church with Dr. Greg Hook officiating.  Interment will be in the Alva Municipal Cemetery under the direction of Marshall Funeral Home of Alva.  
Virgil Lyle Murrow, son of the late Virgil Homer and Iva Amanda (Benson) Murrow, was born June 22, 1934, on the family farm west of Alva, and passed away May 25, 2010, at Alva, Oklahoma, at the age of 75 years, 11 months, and 3 days.  
Lyle graduated from Farry High School with the Class of 1952 and attended Oklahoma A&amp;M at Stillwater.  On March 15, 1956, he was united in marriage to Pearl Marie Utterback at the Methodist Chapel in Alva.  They lived on the farm west of Alva where they farmed and ranched.  
He was a member of the First Baptist Church in Alva and the National Rifle Association.  His family was always very important to him and he enjoyed spending time with them.  He liked hunting, fishing, and riding horses, especially Jake.  He was known as the best cowboy in the country.  
Besides his parents, he was preceded in death by one daughter, Rita Darlene Bryer, and one sister, Mary Katherin Busse.  
Lyle is survived by his wife of 54 years, Pearl, of Alva; two sons, Randy Leon Murrow of Grand Forks, North Dakota and Virgil Ivan Murrow of Alva; one daughter, Regina Harlene Mahon and husband, Rick of Alva; and one son-in-law, Carl Bryer of Woodward.  
Also surviving are 13 grandchildren, Seth Bryer, Amanda Hardy, Rachel Bryer, Levi Bryer, Steven Murrow, Tessa Wilson, Kenny Mahon, Beth Rose, Chelsy Treece, Chase Moore, Shane Murrow, Courtney Murrow, and Carly Murrow; 19 great grandchildren; other relatives and many friends.  
Memorial contributions may be made through the funeral home to the American Cancer Society or the American Heart Association.
