Funeral services for Barbara Thomas will be 3:00 p.m., July 30, 2011, at the Carmen Christian Church with Rev. Kirk Board officiating. Interment will be in the Carmen City Cemetery under the direction of Wentworth Mortuary, LLC, of Carmen. 	 Barbara Lee Thomas, daughter of the late Clifford Leo and Rosie Murl (Lesperance)  Boxley, was born October 11, 1935, at Geary, Oklahoma, and passed away July 27, 2011, at her residence in Jones, Oklahoma, at the age of 75 years, 9 months, and 16 days. 	 Barbara attended school in Geary and graduated from the American School of Chicago, Illinois, in 1954.  She attended college at Seminole Junior College and studied accounting.  On August 29, 1951, she was united in marriage to Edward Ellis Thomas in Oklahoma City. To this union four children were born: Sharon Kay, Debra Rose, Edward Lee, and Michael Ellis. As a military family they lived many places such as: Morocco, Spain, Philippines, New York, Florida, Kansas, Texas, and Oklahoma. She started her own business doing bookkeeping and taxes in Shawnee, Oklahoma, for 18 years before selling her business in 1995.  In 1997, she and Ed traveled full time in their RV before moving back to Edmond in 2009. 	 Besides her parents, she was preceded in death by her husband, Ed, on  March 18, 2011. 	 Barbara is survived by two sons, Edward Thomas of Quanah, Texas, and Michael Thomas of Edmond; two daughters, Sharon Fitzpatrick of Tampa, Florida, and Debra Clark of Edmond; three brothers, Billy Joe Lawrence of Shawnee, Oklahoma, Robert Boxley of Columbia, Missouri, and David Boxley of Kinston, Washington; one sister, Betty Lou Pinkard of Dothan, Alabama; 6 grandchildren, Michelle, Heather, Chris, Heidi, Matt, and Ashley; 14 great grandchildren; other relatives and friends. 	 Memorial contributions may be made through the funeral home to the Norman Veterans Center.
