
					
				Memorial services for Harley William Solomon will be 3:00 p.m. Thursday, September 19, 2013, at Generations in Waynoka with John O’Keefe officiating.  He was cremated at his request.    
Harley William Solomon, son of the late Floyd Raymond and Imogene Elizabeth (Williams) Solomon, was born May 4, 1950, in Waynoka, Oklahoma, and passed away September 14, 2013, at Alva, Oklahoma, at the age of 63 years, 4 months, and 10 days.    
After Harley graduated from high school, he joined the United States Marines.  After completing his tour with the Marines, he served twenty years in the National Guard.  He graduated from Palomar Community College with a degree in Criminal Justice. He worked as a heavy equipment operator with San Diego Gas and Electric until his retirement.  He moved to Waynoka in 1999 and started Harley’s Neon Moon Saloon.    
He was a member of the American Legion.  He enjoyed riding motorcycles, telling stories, and dancing.  He loved and respected people and tried to help others in any way he could.    
Besides his parents, he was preceded in death by one son, Wade Wesley Solomon; and one brother, Randy Solomon.    
Harley is survived by one son, Keith Solomon and wife, Elizabeth, of Waynoka; his daughter, Lisa Solomon, of Orangevale, California; one brother, Terry Solomon of Waynoka; one sister, Toni Solomon-Gate and husband, Bob, of Citrus Heights, California; four grandchildren, Michael, Thomas, Christopher, and Brooklyn Solomon; many other relatives and friends.    
Memorial contributions may be made to the funeral home to help with funeral expenses.
