Funeral services for Paul Lyle Wheat will be 10:00 a.m. Friday, August 13, 2010, at the Alva First United Methodist Church with Rev. Earl Metcalf officiating.  Interment will be in the Alva Municipal Cemetery under the direction of Marshall Funeral Home of Alva.  
Paul Lyle Wheat, son of Logan Blaine and Elizabeth Ann (Carey) Wheat, was born June 10, 1927, in Capron, Oklahoma, and passed away August 10, 2010, in Woodward, Oklahoma, at the age of 83 years and 2 months.  
Paul graduated high school.  On October 12, 1945, he was united in marriage to Betty Lee Rhoades at Medicine Lodge, Kansas.  He served in the 45th Signal Company in the United States Army, earning the Korean Service Medal with one Bronze Star, and Army Occupation Medal (Japan) and the UN Service Medal.  They lived in Alva where they owned and operated Wheat?s Western Store for 57 years.  He was well known for his leather work, making everything from belts and boots to saddles and repairing any and everything.  They moved to Woodward last year.  
Paul was a member of the Dacoma Church of God.  He enjoyed carpentry work, hunting, and most of all his family and God.  
Besides his parents, he was preceded in death by two sons, Gary Wayne and Troy Don Wheat; three brothers, Samuel, Walter, and Blaine Wheat; and five sisters, Edith Schomeik, Nellie Wheat, Dorothy Crouch, Grace Wheat, and Elizabeth "Beth? Zoth.  
Paul is survived by his wife, Betty, of Woodward; one daughter, Paula Marie Miller and husband, William, of Woodward; two grandchildren, Shannon Chestnut and husband, Craig, of Mustang, and Karrie Kay and husband, Kendal, of Ashland, Kansas; six great grandchildren, Jace and Jayden Chestnut of Mustang, and Sloan, Reece, Braton, and Kinley Kay of Ashland, Kansas; other relatives and many friends.  
Memorial contributions may be made through the funeral home to the Dacoma Church of God.
