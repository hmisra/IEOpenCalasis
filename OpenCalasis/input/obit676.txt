
					
				Funeral services for Ralph Frank Rauch will be 10:30 a.m. Saturday, December 8, 2012, at the Garland Road Baptist Church in Enid with Dr. Greg Hook officiating.  Interment will be 2:30 p.m. Saturday at the Memorial Hill Cemetery north of Waynoka under the direction of Marshall Funeral Home of Waynoka, LLC.    
Ralph Rauch, son of the late Albin Martin and Pearl Mae (Rawson) Rauch, was born November 19, 1931, on the farm north of Waynoka, and passed away December 5, 2012, at El Reno, Oklahoma, at the age of 81 years and 16 days.    
Ralph attended a rural school north of Waynoka, graduated from Waynoka High School, and attended Northwestern Oklahoma State University.  He served in the United States Army.  He was united in marriage to Betty Cook at Waynoka.    
He started working at the ice plant in Waynoka until it closed.  He then worked at Gage at a feedmill, then at in Texas at a feed mills, before returning to Waynoka where he worked at the Deep Rock for eight years.  He then worked as a mechanic at Enid and Yukon until an injury forced his retirement.  He returned to Enid until recently moving to El Reno two months ago.    
Besides his parents, he was preceded in death by one brother, Leon Rauch; and two sisters, Opal Walls and Ruby Baker.    
Ralph is survived by his wife, Betty, of El Reno; two sons, Rickey Rauch and wife, Sheila, of El Reno, and Terry Rauch of Carrier; one daughter Denise Weber and husband, Fred, of North Carolina; numerous grand children, great grandchildren, and great great grandchildren; other relatives and friends.    
In lieu of flowers, memorial contributions may be made to the funeral home to help with funeral expenses.    
Remembrances may be shared with the family at www.marshallfuneralhomes.com.
