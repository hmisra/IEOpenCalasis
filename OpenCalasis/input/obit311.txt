Funeral services for Janice Eileen (Clemence) Riboni will be 10:00 a.m. Saturday, July 24, 2010, at the Waynoka Church of God with Rev. Ken Higgins officiating.  Interment will be in the Waynoka Municipal Cemetery under the direction of Marshall Funeral Home of Waynoka, LLC.  
Janice Eileen (Clemence) Riboni, daughter of Esther Ruth (Woolley) Clemence and the late Clyde Franklin Clemence, was born October 7, 1939, at Waynoka, Oklahoma, and passed away July 20, 2010, at Springfield, Missouri, at the age of 70 years, 9 months, and 13 days.  
After Janice graduated from Waynoka High School she attended Northwestern State College.  On December 19, 2010, she was united in marriage to Douglas P. Taylor in Waynoka.  They owned and operated the Dairy Maid in Waynoka for many years.  Her parents built the grocery store in Waynoka which she owned and intermittently operated until she sold it about 8 years ago.  In November 1975, she was united in marriage to Mario Riboni in Waynoka.  
She was a member of the First Church of God.  She enjoyed shopping.  
Besides her parents, she was preceded in  dath by her husband, Mario.  
Janice is survived by two daughters, Lanas Kaytryn Long of Waynoka, and LaJan Kayle Taylor of Galena, Missouri; her mother, Esther Clemence of Waynoka; one brother, Eldon Clemence and wife, Norma, of Garden City, Kansas; one grandson, Hanson Long of Waynoka; other relatives and many friends.  
Memorial contributions may be made through the funeral home to the Oklahoma Medical Research Foundation for cancer research.
