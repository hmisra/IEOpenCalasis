Funeral services for Richard Dean Roots will be 2:00 p.m. Saturday, February 6, 2010, at the Hardtner United Methodist Church with Pastor Elizabeth Kirkwood officiating.  Interment will be in the Hardtner Cemetery under the direction of Marshall Funeral Home of Alva.  
Richard Dean Roots, son of the late Ralph Wesley and Mildred Esther (Nicholson) Roots, was born May 13, 1930, at Wilmore, Kansas, and passed away February 3, 2010, at Enid, Oklahoma, at the age of 79 years, 8 months, and 20 days.  
After Richard graduated from Hardtner High School, he served in the United States Army where he was a construction engineer.  On May 28, 1953, he was united in marriage to Beverly Jo Daughhetee at Medicine Lodge, Kansas.  They lived in Holcomb, Kansas for 25 years where he served on the City Council and the Recreation Commission.  In 1985, they moved to Cherokee.  He worked as a carpenter all of his life, often doing small jobs for the elderly for free.  
He was a member of the American Legion Post #0175 at Hardtner, the Veteran of Foreign Wars at Garden City, and the Fraternal Order of Eagles in Garden City since 1967.  He loved fishing, particularly at Great Plaines Lake and enjoyed the company of his grandpups, Rose Marie and Annalisa Mae.  
Besides his parents, he was preceded in death by an infant son; and one brother, Raymond Roots in 1951.  
Richard is survived by his wife, Beverly, of Cherokee; one daughter, Yolanda Roots of Cherokee; one brother Russell Roots and wife, Celia, of Alva; other relatives and many friends.  
Memorial contributions may be made through the funeral home to the Hardtner American Legion.
