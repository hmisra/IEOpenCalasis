Funeral services for Olive Moore Coyle, age 97, will be 2:00 p.m. Thursday, December 21, 2006, at the Alva Church of God with Reverend Bob Brown and Reverend Ken Bowden officiating.  Interment will be in the Alva Municipal Cemetery under the direction of Marshall Funeral Home of Alva.  
Olive Moore Coyle, daughter of the late Charles and Rosa (Koehn) Howland was born December 26, 1908, 14 miles north and 5Â« miles west of Alva, in Woods County, Oklahoma, and passed away, December 18, 2006, in Kiowa, Kansas, at the age of 97 years, 11 months, and 22 days.  
Olive attended grade school at Rose Hill #117 and graduated from Hardtner High School with the Class of 1927.  She attended college at Alva earning her teaching certificate.  She taught at Rose Hill for one year.  In July, 1929, she was united in marriage to Floyd "Skeet? Moore at Alva.  To this union one daughter was born.  They enjoyed working with race horses and traveled extensively throughout the United States.  
In the spring of 1950, the family moved to Highmore, South Dakota and in 1970, moved back to Alva.  Mr. Moore passed away in 1981 and in 1989 she was married to Andy Coyle of Waynoka.  Olive was involved in helping  start the Woods County Senior Citizens organization.  They moved to Kiowa, Kansas in 2002.  
Olive gave her life to God while in high school, later joining the United Brethren Church in Alva.  She later joined and was baptized at the Alva Church of God.  
Besides her parents and her husband, Skeet, she was preceded in death by her daughter, Yvonne, in 1970; two sisters; one step daughter, and an adopted daughter, Ann Fox.  
Olive is survived by her husband, Andy of Kiowa; five step children; several grandchildren, great-grandchildren and great-great grandchildren, one nephew and a host of friends.  
Memorial contributions may be made through the funeral home to the Woods County Senior Citizens Center or the Kiowa Senior Citizens Center.
