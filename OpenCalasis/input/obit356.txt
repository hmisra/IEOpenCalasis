Funeral services for Marvin Leroy Turner will be 10:30 a.m. Friday, September 5, 2008 at the Alva Municipal Cemetery with Brenda Johnson officiating.  Interment will be in the Alva Municipal Cemetery under the direction of Marshall Funeral Home of Alva.  
Marvin Leroy Turner, son of the late Glen Kenneth and Jewel Suphronia (Lucas) Turner, was born April 9, 1931, at Bentonville, Arkansas, and passed away September 2, 2008, at Oklahoma City, Oklahoma, at the age of 77 years, 4 months, and 23 days.  
Marvin attended Miller Rural School near Bentonville, Arkansas.  He served in the United States Army and served in Korea during the Korean Conflict.  After his discharge from the service, he worked as a long haul trucker, traveling from coast to coast and into Canada.    
On May 19, 1962, he was united in marriage to Beverly Joyce Smith in Cimarron, Kansas.  They made their home in Lyons, Kansas, where he worked as  a carpenter until his retirement.  In 1998, they moved to their present home in Alva, Oklahoma.  It was there where Marvin became a grandfather of three beautiful girls who called him "Papa.?  They were the greatest joy in his life.  
Marvin was baptized into the Baptist Church.  He was a member of the Veteran?s of Foreign Wars.  He was an avid fisherman and also enjoyed hunting.  
Besides his parents, he was preceded in death by two brothers, Don Turner and Billy Turner; and two sisters, Glenda Faye Duntz and Ruby Parrish.  
Marvin is survived by his wife, Beverly, of Alva; one daughter, Marlene Joyce Swenn and husband, Terry, of Alva; one sister, Barbara Simpson of Pea Ridge, Arkansas; three grandchildren, Emily Rose Swenn, Lacie Lyn Swenn, and Julie Ann Swenn, all of Alva; other relatives and friends.  
Memorial contributions may be made through the funeral home to the Oklahoma Medical Research Foundation for kidney disease research.
