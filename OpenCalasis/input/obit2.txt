Funeral services for Daniel Lynde Curtis will be 2:00 p.m. Wednesday, August 24, 2011, at the First Christian Church with Reverend Charles Newton officiating.  Interment will be in the Waynoka Municipal Cemetery under the direction of Marshall Funeral Home of Waynoka, LLC.  
Daniel Lynde Curtis, son of the late Fred Lynde and Opal  Marguerrittie (McCoy) Curtis, was born March 25, 1946, at Hardtner, Kansas, and passed away at his home north of Waynoka August 21, 2011, at the age of 65 years, 4 months, and 26 days.  
Danny attended Avard School through the 3rd grade and attended Waynoka High School.  On July 26, 1966, he was inducted into the United States Army.  He served as a Heavy Vehicle Driver with the Eighth Army, and served 13 months in Vietnam.  
Danny started truck driving in 1968.  He had a ranch in Stark City, Missouri, where he lived 10 years, then moved to Dumas, Texas where he lived 10 years.  On May 21, 1988, he was united in marriage to Terry (Smith) Curtis at Dumas, Texas.  They returned to Waynoka where he drove a truck for Litzenberger Construction.  
Danny was confirmed at the Avard Christian Church on March 3, 1958, and baptized on April 6, 1958.  He enjoyed fished, hunting and taking care of his horses.  
Besides his parents, he was preceded in death by one brother, Jack Michael Curtis, and one sister, Marcheta Letitia Dunn.  
He is survived by his wife, Terry, of Waynoka; one daughter, Danna Farley and husband, Kevin, of Keller, Texas, one son, Daniel Curtis, Jr. of Carmen, Oklahoma; one step daughter, Amanda Thorp and husband, Melton, of Sonora, Texas; one step son, Jeremiah Dubiel of Bartlett, Texas; three grandchildren; Blake Farley, Abby Farley, and Payton Farley; three step grandchildren, Ian Thorp, Myranda Thorp, and Katelyn Thorp; other relatives and many friends.  
Memorial contributions may be made through the funeral home to Oklahoma Medical Research Foundation for cancer research.
