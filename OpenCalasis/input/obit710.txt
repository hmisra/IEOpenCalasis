Graveside services for Mary Lou Witchey will be 2:00 p.m. Monday, February 1, 2010, at the Whitehorse Cemetery with the Reverend Paul Cole officiating.  Interment will follow  under the direction of Marshall Funeral Home of Alva.  
Mary Lou Witchey, daughter of the late Floyd Elmer and Blanche (Crabb) Witchey, was born April 16, 1920, at Whitehorse, Oklahoma, and passed away January 28, 2010, at Alva, Oklahoma, at the age of 89 years, 9 months, and 12 days.  
Mary attended grade school at Pleasant Ridge, Whitehorse, and Farry.  In 1933, the family moved to Alva where Mary attended Alva High School, graduating with the Class of 1938.  In 1942 she went to Salem, Oregon where she made her home.  She worked in a large department store as ladies sportswear Department Manager until 1961 when she moved to Oakdale, California to be near her father who was in ill health.  He passed away in 1969, at which time she returned to Alva to make her home.  She worked for Singer Sewing Company, then TG&amp;Y, retiring when the store closed in Alva.  
Besides her parents, she was preceded in death by two sisters, Pauline Holt and Anna Allison; and three brothers, Charles, Donald and John Witchey.  
Mary is survived by five brothers, Billy Witchey of Alva, Robert Witchey and wife, Vada, of Alva, Dean Witchey of Alva, Dale Witchey of Alva, and Earl Witchey and wife, Mavis, of Webster, South Dakota; one sister, Gail Wilks and husband, Bill, of Alva; other relatives and friends.  
Memorial contributions may be made through the funeral home to the American Heart Association.
