Graveside services for Sammy Claude Morris will be 3:00 p.m. Saturday, May 17, 2008, at the Waynoka Municipal Cemetery with Rev. Marvin Stuckey, pastor of the Waynoka First United Methodist Church officiating. Interment is under the direction of Marshall Funeral Home of Waynoka, LLC.  There will be a visitation with the family prior to the service on Saturday from 1:30 to 2:30 at Marshall Funeral Home of  Waynoka.  
Sammy Claude Morris, son of the late Clyde L. and Minnie Ethel (Boswell) Morris, was born April 7, 1924, at Ninnekah, Oklahoma, and passed away May 15, 2008, at Woodward, Oklahoma, at the age of 84 years and 21 days.  
Sammy graduated from Alex high School. After graduating he served almost three years in the United States Army. In 1946, he was united in marriage to Ann Morris Faudion in Germany. He then went to work as a heavy equipment operator in the pipeline industry. He was united in marriage to Leona Wilson in Canton, Texas. He followed his job to Mississippi, New Mexico, and several places in Oklahoma before settling in Woodward in 1973.  
He was a member of the Alex/Chickasha Masonic Lodge. Work was his life and he really enjoyed having a beautiful yard at his home.  
Besides his parents, he was preceded in death by his wife, Leona, in 1988; brothers and sisters, Harvey Hart, Alfred Hart, George Morris, Woodrow Morris, Hellen Smith, Dora Plamer, and Dorthy Morris.  
Sammy is survived by three sons, Sammy Morris of Drumright, Oklahoma, Larry Morris and his wife, Connie, of Bradley, Oklahoma, and Wayne Morris and his wife, Cindi, of Woodward, Oklahoma; and three step children, Ronnie Howell, Rodney Howell, and LaStella Howell; five brothers and sisters, Ollie Thomas, Hollis Morris, Ruth Manika, Cora Manika, and Billy Morris; five grandchildren, Jenny, Sherry, Sammi, Dana, and Shawna; other relatives and friends.  
Memorial contributions may be made through the funeral home to the Oklahoma Medical Research Foundation for heart research.
