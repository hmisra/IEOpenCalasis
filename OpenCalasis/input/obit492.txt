Funeral services for Linda Sue Penner will be 2:00 p.m. Saturday, November 3, 2007, at the Waynoka First United Methodist Church with Pastor Marvin Stuckey officiating.  Interment will be in the Waynoka Municipal Cemetery under the direction of Marshall Funeral Home of Waynoka, LLC.  
Linda Sue Penner, daughter of the late Eldred Alanson and Eula Mae (Devilbiss) Pierce, was born May 21, 1940, at Cleo Springs, Oklahoma, and passed away October 30, 2007, at Tulsa, Oklahoma, at the age of 67 years.  
After Sue graduated from Waynoka High School, she attended Northwestern Oklahoma State University.  She then attended beauty school and worked as a beautician most of her life.  In 1981, she moved from Waynoka to Enid, and eventually to Oologah in 1997.  
She was a member of the First United Methodist Church.  She greatly enjoyed visiting with her family and playing with her grandchildren.  She enjoyed jewelry making, sewing, quilting, reading, and cooking.  
Besides her parents, she was preceded in death by one sister, Shirley Steichen.  
Sue is survived by her daughter, Kimberly Sue Cooper and her husband, Bill, of Oologah; two grandchildren, Tara and Tyler Cooper of Oologah; nine brothers and sisters, Gene Pierce and his wife, Doris, of Napa, California, Karol Pierce and his wife, Claudette, of Baltimore, Ohio, Dixie Stinson of Norman, Oklahoma, Ross Pierce and his wife, Ann, of Enid, Oklahoma, Collin Pierce and his wife, Dot, of Freedom, Oklahoma, Larry Pierce and his wife, Carrie, of Yukon, Oklahoma, Garry Pierce and his wife, Toni, of Collinsville, Oklahoma, Eula Augustin of Nashville, Tennessee, and Merle Marrow of Tulsa, Oklahoma; numerous nieces and nephews; other relatives and many friends.  
Memorial contributions may be made through the funeral home to the American Cancer Society.
