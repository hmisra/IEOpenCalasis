Funeral services for Stella Marie "Dolly" Woolley, 89 will be 10:30 a.m. Thursday, July 20, 2006, at the Waynoka Church of God with Rev. Charles Newton officiating.  Interment will be in the Waynoka Municipal Cemetery under the direction of Marshall Funeral Home of Waynoka.  
Stella Marie "Dolly" Woolley, daughter of the late Joe Virgil and Stella Mae (Icke) Neff, was born December 25, 1916, at Alva, Oklahoma, and passed away July 17, 2006, at the Waynoka Nursing Home at the age of 89 years, 6 months, and 22 days.  
Dolly attended Morning Star School.  On July 5, 1960, she was united in marriage to Claude Woolley at Waynoka, Oklahoma.  She worked as a cook at Miller&amp;s Cafe?Ã¿?for 40 years.  
She was a member of the Morning Star Baptist Church.  She enjoyed quilting, gardening, and needle point.  
Besides her parents, she was preceded in death by her husband, Claude; one daughter, Leona Morris, and infant twins, Billy Joe and Anna May; one brother, Dwight Neff; and one sister, Bessie Phillips.  
Dolly is survived by three sons, Kenneth Wilson of Okemah, Oklahoma, Richard Wilson and his wife, Mary, of Clarion, Pennsylvania, and Edward Wilson and his wife, Julie, of Magnolia, Texas; one daughter, Claudette Pierce and her husband, Carol, of Columbus, Ohio; one sister, Margaret Windecker of Waynoka; 17 grandchildren, numerous great grandchildren, great great grandchildren and other relatives and many friends.  
Memorial contributions may be made through the funeral home to the Waynoka Nursing Home.
