Charles Mason "Buck" Ingraham, son of the late Charles Frederick and Jane (Million) Ingraham, was born January 24, 1921, in Clayton, New Mexico, and passed away June 3, 2006, at Enid, Oklahoma, at the age of 88 years, 4 months, and 9 days.  
On February 8, 1941, he was united in marriage to Leona Harper at Alva, Oklahoma.  To this union three children, Keith, Kenneth, and Charlene were born. They lived on a farm for a few years before he went to work as a welder for the Santa Fe Railroad.  After his retirement in 1982, they lived in Kiowa for a short time before moving to Alva in 1984.  
He liked bowling and playing pitch in their pitch club.  They were also members of a campers club and spent the winters in south Texas for 15 years.  
Besides his parents, he was preceded in death by one son, Kenneth, one great-grandson, Joshua; four brothers, Lewis, Bob, Curly, and Walt; and one sister, Verle Pedtrotta.  
Buck is survived by his wife, Leona, of Alva; one son, Keith Ingraham and his wife, Sue, of Alva; one daughter, Charlene Campbell of Alva; 7 grandchildren; 15 great grandchildren; other relatives and friends.  
Funeral services will be 10:00 a.m. Wednesday, June 7, 2006, at the Alva Church of God with pastor Bob Brown officiating.  Interment will be in the Alva Municipal Cemetery under the direction of Marshall Funeral Home of Alva.  
Memorial contributions may be made through the funeral home to the Alva Church of God.
