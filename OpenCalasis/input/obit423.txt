Memorial services for Delmer Lewis Brune will be 2:00 p.m. Friday, March 22, 2013, at the Waynoka First Baptist Church with Rev. David Shaw officiating.  Delmer was cremated at his request under the direction of Marshall Funeral Home of Waynoka, LLC.    
Delmer Lewis Brune, son of the late Elmer and Gertrude Anna (Rauh) Brune, was born February 12, 1943, at Cherokee, Oklahoma, and passed away March 18, 2013, at Alva, Oklahoma, at the age of 70 years, 1 month, and 6 days.    
Delmer attended Freehome School and Emerson School before graduating from Enid High School.  He also attended New Mexico State University.  On November 10, 1964, he was united in marriage to Mary Elizabeth Cunningham at Enid, Oklahoma.  He served as a cook in the Oklahoma National Guard for eight years.  They lived on the farm near Waynoka where he was well known as a jack of all trades.    
He attended the Lutheran Church.  He was a member of the Rodeo Committee and helped with a lot of the Waynoka Chamber of Commerce activities.  He enjoyed hunting and fishing.    
Besides his parents, he was preceded in death by his brother, John Brune and his sister, Caroline Brune.    
Delmer is survived by his wife, Mary, of Waynoka; two sons, Phillip Brune and wife, Kama, of Joplin, Missouri, and Quinten Brune of Waynoka; one daughter, Anna Wilcox and husband, Justin, of Freedom; three brothers, G. J. Brune and wife, Marlene, of Enid, Paul Brune and wife, Ramona, of Lawton; and Carl Brune and wife, Julia, of Tulsa; 15 grandchildren; 1 great grandchild; other relatives and many friends.    
Memorial contributions may be made through the funeral home to the Oklahoma Medical Research Foundation for research on heart disease.    
Remembrances may be shared with the family at www.marshallfuneralhomes.com.
